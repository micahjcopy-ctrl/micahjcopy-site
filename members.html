This month's send map

July → August: the summer slump is a segmentation problem

Summer buyers are outdoors, traveling, and allergic to hard pitches. Volume down is fine. Relevance down is not. Every email answers one question: how does this make their summer better?

July · Week 3

Post-holiday reset. One "reply back" engagement email to your 90-day segment (question, not pitch). Replies repair deliverability while everyone else blasts clearance.

July · Week 4

Clear summer inventory with bundles framed around use ("the [routine/trip/season] kit"), not discounts. Soft urgency: end-of-month, honest reason attached.

August · Week 1

Routine-reset angle. Back-to-school energy applies to every category: schedules return, habits restart. "September you will thank August you" positioning for consumables and subscriptions.

August · Week 2

Proof week. One case-study or customer-story email, zero CTA beyond a soft P.S. This is the give-give-give beat that buys your Labor Day ask.

August · Weeks 3–4

Labor Day build. Tease early access to your engaged segment first, run the sale to everyone after. Early-access-to-engaged is the highest-ROI move of the month.
