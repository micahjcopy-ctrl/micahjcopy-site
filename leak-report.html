A true story about how this happens to good brands

A brand I work with — strong product, six-figure list — watched email revenue drop 42% in one month. The team blamed the list. "It's burnt." "People don't read email anymore."

The real cause: during a redesign, someone paused the welcome flow. The proven one — it had earned over $1M lifetime. Its replacement was "live" but under-triggering, reaching a fraction of new signups. The cart-abandon flow? Also paused, for months. Nobody checked, because dashboards don't announce what isn't sending.

That pattern — not bad copy, not a dead list — is the most common revenue leak in DTC email. And it's mechanical to find and mechanical to fix, if you know where to look.
